ABOUT


My name is Brendan Walsh. I am a student at Michigan State University who is studying computer science and game design. For the longest time, I have been interested in technology, and more specifically, video games. In high school, I decided that I wanted to try my hand at game development and set out to learn the required skills to do so. I started teaching myself several relevant pieces of game development, such as 3D modeling, level design, and of course, programming. The language I started out with was Python, and I was instantly captivated. I made simple text games at first and kept building up my skills from there. I then moved on to messing around with game engines, such as Unity, Unreal, and Source. I began taking courses for computer science and game design at my high school. I knew that I had found my passion and my path in life, and it was to become programmer. Over the years, I have worked on many projects, both in school and out of school, I have attended many hackathons, I have done all I can to further my knowledge in this amazing field.

Several years have passed since I chose to become a programmer, and I am now a senior at Michigan State University. Soon I will enter the real world and begin putting my skills to use. I hope that I can make the world a better place, and that I can continue to learn even more.

This website was built as a means of teaching myself web design. I had some basic knowledge of HTML, CSS, and Javascript before, but I never really got deep into it, until now.

Also, you may be wondering "Beedzo? What's a Beedzo?" Well... it was my childhood nickname.

Projects







Best Worst Wheel

A Wheel of Fortune style graphical selection wheel. The user spins it, and it will eventually come to a stop on an item on the wheel. The user can add or delete items from the wheel, as well as spin it. Items that are added are stored in a local SQLite database, so they are loaded again when the user closes and reopens the program.

Wingman From the Other Side

Voted as one of the most innovative game projects of MI445 in Fall semester 2019, Wingman from the Other Side is a physics based puzzle game built with Unity engine. You play as Laramie the ghost, whose job is to possess objects in each level to bring a couple together. I provided a lot of the underlying C# programming for the game's systems, and also helped with various other aspects of the game. I particularly enjoyed working on this game, and had a great experience with the team I worked on it with.

Cretaceous Companion

Cretaceous Companion is a game I helped create in my Intro to Game Design and Development class. I mostly handled programming but helped out in various other aspects of the game as well. This was the second group project of the class, and of the 8 games submitted by the whole class, was voted to be the most fun game. Cretaceous Companion was also chosen as an "Outstanding Game Project" in Fall 2019.
